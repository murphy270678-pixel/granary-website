Local recommendations

Best things to do on the Isle of Skye.

See the famous landscapes, but leave room for the places that reward a slower and quieter day.

The classic Skye experiences

The Old Man of Storr, Fairy Pools, Quiraing, Kilt Rock and Portree harbour are popular for good reason. Visit early or later in the day where possible, and treat one or two major sights as a full day rather than racing between a checklist.

Quieter walks worth the effort

Brothers’ Point

A clifftop walk near Culnaknock with coastal views and a natural arch. It is a strong alternative when the Storr feels too busy, although the path can still be wet and rough.

Rubha Hunish

Walk towards Skye’s northernmost point for wide sea views and a sense of space. Take care around cliffs and be ready for fast-changing weather.

MacLeod’s Maidens

Three dramatic sea stacks viewed from a longer farm-track and coastal walk near Idrigill Point. The distance keeps it much quieter than roadside viewpoints.

Trumpan Church

A ruined church in Waternish with clifftop views towards the Outer Hebrides. Many visitors turn back before reaching this far north-west.

Food and craft stops we recommend

Try Lean To Coffee near Ashaig, Caledonian Cafe or The Granary in Portree, and Cuchullin for Scottish produce and seafood. For working crafts, Skye Silver and Edinbane Pottery are worthwhile stops. Check current opening days before making a special journey.

When it rains

Consider a pre-booked Talisker Distillery tour, Dunvegan Castle and Gardens during its seasonal opening, or Skyeskyns in Waternish. Portree also works well for a slower café-and-harbour day.

Our honest adviceSkye is better when you do less. Pick an area for the day, allow time for single-track roads and keep one wet-weather alternative ready.
